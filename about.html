Principal Architect

Suresh Kumar Bandaru

Suresh Kumar Bandaru is the founding principal and chief architect of Sai Nirmaan Architects. With over two decades of professional practice, Suresh has shaped a diverse portfolio spanning residential, commercial, institutional, and humanitarian architecture across South India and Odisha.

Suresh holds a Bachelor of Architecture from SAR College of Architecture (Affiliated to JNAFAU), a Post-Graduate Diploma in Environmental Studies, and a Post-Graduate Diploma in Town and Country Planning. His commitment to human-centered design and community impact is evident in landmark projects including the Tribal Museum (Odisha, Design Proposal 2023) and the Indoor Stadium for SAAP. He is also deeply involved in the DRES (Disaster-Resilient & Enclosed Shelter) programme, partnering with CARE India, Lutheran World Service, and Save the Children on humanitarian housing initiatives in disaster-affected coastal communities.

Our Philosophy

Architecture that serves people first

At Sai Nirmaan Architects, we believe that the best architecture is human-centered. Every design decision is made with the end user in mind — how they move through a space, how it makes them feel, how it impacts their daily lives. We reject the notion that beauty and function are at odds. Instead, we craft spaces that are elegant, purposeful, and deeply rooted in their cultural and environmental context.

Whether designing a family home, a civic institution, or disaster-resilient settlements, we bring the same rigor, empathy, and attention to detail. Our work is a conversation between place, people, and purpose.

Good architecture doesn't shout — it quietly improves the life of everyone who passes through it.

— Suresh Kumar Bandaru
